Sleep and Eating Schedules:

Due to the current situation, many people do not maintain regular schedules for sleeping and eating. Use these tips to manage your schedules!

Sleep tips:

1. Sleep for at least 7 to 9 hours a day to get enough energy to have an abudance of energy the next day!
2. Try to maintain a regular sleep schedule by sleeping at the same time each day around 9 PM - 11 PM and wake up around the same time as well.
3. To keep your circadium cycle going, get lots of sunlight during the day, and use blackout curtains or eye masks to keep the light out during night. This helps release more sleep hormones (melatonin)
4. Avoid electronics that emit lots of blue light or buy these nifty sunglasses that block against blue light!
5. Check out this chronotype test to know your sleep type and to maximize productivity based on your sleep type! Lean more about chronotypes here.
6. Get earplugs or close the door to prevent noise from getting to your brain because even when you're sleeping, your brain still processes noise!
7. The material of your bed is important, but it's not very practical to buy a new one. Of course, if you want to, feel free to do so! A better way to feel comfortable in bed is to get a nice pillow made out of natural fibers such as wool, down, cotton, linen, and silk. Make sure your bedding is clean and wash regularly!
8. If you are hungry before bed, try eating a light snack less than 200 calories.
9. Check this and this out for more information!

1. Many people are overeating. To prevent that, only eat when you are hungry and at mealtimes. You only should have 3 meals a day, with at most two light snacks.
2. Don't eat at night because you won't be active enough at night to burn the calories. Eating snacks during the afternoon is better. Also not eating 2 hours before sleeptime also helps you sleep. However you shouldn't go to sleep hungry either. Check out sleep tip #8!
3. Sometimes you will mistake your thirst as hunger. If it's not time to eat, drink water if you think you're hungry. Drinking water also helps you stay hydrated!
4. Don't eat while doing something else. This distracts your brain and you'll often continue to eat even if you're full. That causes overeating.
5. Eat one bite at a time, and eat slowly so you have time to digest and your brain can realize that you're full.
6. Eat a healthy portion of fruit, veggie, protein, dairy, etc and avoid junk food (of course you can enjoy junk food once in a while :P).
